About BAS

    Welcome to Banking Analysis System...!. As Banking System becomes more digitized, customers still crave a human connection. Today, customers are able to quickly raise their issues through social media, and this digital economy, an empowered consumer and expanding regulatory scrutiny are putting great pressure on banks to adapt with new solutions and new business models. So, we did some analysis to show how twitter is being used by banks and how people are engaged in knowing each and every update of the banks.

What we did....!

    Twitter is a fabulous source for information. Whenever something is happening, people around the world start tweeting away. We used twitter as source of information for analyzing the data with some queries to provide logical output and then visualizing them to understandable pictorial representations.
    Apache Spark™ is a fast and general engine for large-scale data processing. Spark offers over 80 high-level operators that make it easy to build parallel apps. And you can use it interactively from the Scala, Python and R shells and you can write applications quickly in Java, Scala, Python, R.

Steps Involved

We used Spark locally in our Java process space. We created Sparkconf object which points to our Spark instance as in this case 'local' and in order to interact with Spark we need a SparkContext instance, so we created JavaSparkContext.

Now, the steps involved are:
  • Load our input data.
  • Parse our input into words.
  • Reduce our words into a tuple pair that contains the word and the count of occurrences.
  • Save our results.
  • Visualizing the results saved with charts and graphs.
